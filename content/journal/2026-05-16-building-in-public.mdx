I almost didn't build this.

For nearly two decades I&rsquo;ve worked in kitchens. The front line first, then the office, then the view across multiple properties at once. Whatever I would have written about myself was beside the point. The work was the story. The pass at six in the morning, the bread coming out of the oven at four, the cost sheet at the end of the month. You don&rsquo;t write about that. You do it.

What changed is that the kitchen got an upgrade.

## The moment everything moved

Sometime in the last eighteen months, a particular kind of operational problem stopped being a multi-week project and started being a one-afternoon conversation. Allergen panels for every outlet. SOPs translated for a Fijian brigade. A recipe library that prints, exports, generates QR codes, and stays in sync across three properties. Procurement RFPs scored against supplier specs. Food cost variance across menus.

None of that work used to be possible without spreadsheets, weeks of nights, and a junior I was reluctant to ask. Now it's a Sunday morning at the kitchen table with coffee, an AI co-pilot, and Sharon's golden retriever Maggie watching the back door.

That shift is what this site is about.

## Who this is for

I'm a Group Executive Chef working across island resorts in Fiji. I run F&B operations for Raffe Hotels & Resorts — a dozen outlets across three properties, a brigade of cooks, suppliers, GMs, and owners. I've also spent the last few years building AI tools for hospitality. ExeChef is one of them.

If you run a kitchen, manage a hotel, lead a brand inside a hospitality group, or just care about how AI changes operational work — this is for you. If you're somewhere else in the FIRE journey, building toward a freer life, or trying to figure out what reinvention looks like at midlife — this is also for you. If you like food and travel and the occasional fishing story — pull up a chair.

> The most valuable thing I own isn't a recipe or a résumé. It's the stack of operational notebooks I've kept across nearly three decades, the patterns inside them, and the quiet belief that everyone can change their own destiny.

## What you'll find here

There's no editorial calendar. There's no funnel. There's no course. This is a public notebook.

What I'll write about:

- **Build Log** — what I'm shipping, how, what broke
- **Hospitality Notes** — the operator's view of the things nobody writes about
- **AI Experiments** — the prompts, agents, and pipelines I'm running
- **Procurement AI** — forecasting, supplier scoring, RFP automation
- **Travel AI** — using agents to design better trips and a freer life
- **ExeChef Updates** — the work behind the recipe system
- **Personal Reflections** — Sharon, Maggie, gratitude, the long arc

## A note on voice

I'm not interested in being an authority. I'm interested in being useful and honest. If something I write is wrong, tell me. If I leave out the difficult part, push me on it. The internet has enough confident people who never tell on themselves.

This site is where some of those notebooks become public. Thanks for being here. There'll be more soon.

— Don
